Gloria Macapagal Ayorro
Born April 5, 1987. Gloria Macapagal Ayorro grew to be one of presidents of Philippines just like her father Diosdo Macapagal. From 2001 - 2010, she fixed the economic meltdown and organized policies.

  • filed a lot of bills and laws.
  • maintained economy.
  • promote rights of women (ex. anti-sexual harrasment law, women economic empowerment).

Jose Rizal
Born June 19, 1861. Jose Rizal had a wealthy and educational childhood in Manila in which he used to combat against the injustices as he was affected under the Spanish colonization for his family and his country.

  • created letters and books against the Spaniards (exposed them and warned Filipinos).
  • His death sparked nationalism among Filipinos during the Spanish Colonial era.
  • nurtured Filipino culture with his stories.

Lapu-Lapu
The group leader or also known as the Sultan of Sulu and the datu in Mactan

  • Successfully defended Philippines early from invaders.

Douglas MacArthur
A general from the American Military during 1945 yet showed his honor and promise in protecting the Philippines.

  • Established an army in the Philippines, especially for against the Japanese invasion.
  • A figure of inspiration and determination during the invasion (Ex. his quote, "I shall return").

Marcela Agoncillo
A Filipina seamtress and the wife of Felipe Agoncillo.

  • Sewn the first Philippine flag during her time in Korea.
  • A symbolization of Filipino pride.
